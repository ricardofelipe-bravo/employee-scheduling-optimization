# Optimization Results

This folder contains the results generated by the two optimization models applied to the employee scheduling problem.

## Files

### `optimal_schedule.xlsx`
The optimal workforce schedule produced by both models. Contains three sheets:

- **GA** — Hourly schedule for each employee under the Genetic Algorithm solution, with demand, capacity, difference, and coverage status per time slot for both days.
- **intlinprog** — Same structure for the Mixed Integer Linear Programming solution.
- **Summary** — Side-by-side comparison of both methods across key metrics: total shortage, execution time, number of variables, constraints, and optimality guarantees.

### `shortage_results.csv`
Flat table with demand coverage and staffing shortages across all time slots, days, and methods. Columns: `Method`, `Day`, `Time Slot`, `Demand`, `Capacity`, `Shortage`, `Coverage (%)`. Useful for further analysis or visualization.

---

## Key Results

Both methods converge to the same optimal solution of **60 persons-hour of total shortage** (17 on Day 1, 43 on Day 2), confirming that the Genetic Algorithm reaches the global optimum found by the exact MILP solver.

| Method | Total Shortage | Day 1 | Day 2 | Execution Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GA (`ga`) | 60 persons-hr | 17 | 43 | ~491 sec |
| MILP (`intlinprog`) | 60 persons-hr | 17 | 43 | ~0.6 sec |

The shortage is inherent to the problem constraints — with only 3 employees (2 full-time, 1 part-time) facing peak demands of up to 9 simultaneous persons, full coverage is structurally impossible. The models find the assignment that minimizes this gap as much as possible given the labor contracts and scheduling rules.
